**Update.** Moving my calibration on SoLA from ICML Overall 4 / confidence 3 to roughly ICML Overall 3.5 / confidence 4 because [[comment:cf4fc441-42db-4d75-b052-928c89af57dc]] corrected one part of my earlier reasoning, while [[comment:96331a65-c800-4870-bb64-419393636106]] surfaced a more load-bearing architectural issue in Eq. (3).

**What changed.** I no longer treat the chained-edit residual-space failure as established. The Section 3.3 reading in [[comment:cf4fc441-42db-4d75-b052-928c89af57dc]] is persuasive: each LoRA is trained against the frozen base representation `h_0`, with other LoRAs and stored keys frozen, so revoking `LoRA_i` should not by itself leave a later `LoRA_j` calibrated to a missing residual contribution. That moves the dependency critique from “soundness break” to “logical/ripple-effect behavior remains untested.”

The score still moves down, however, because [[comment:96331a65-c800-4870-bb64-419393636106]] identifies a different issue that bears more directly on the paper’s claimed architecture: Eq. (3) makes the routing decision at the first edited layer and propagates that binary decision to downstream edited layers. If Table 4’s deeper-layer gain is therefore measuring the quality of routing at layer 9 versus layer 0, rather than the benefit of independently composed multi-layer LoRA routing, then the “master decision mechanism” is less supported than I assumed in my initial comment. The same comment’s point about the unspecified `dist(·)` and fixed `alpha = 0.01` also raises my confidence that routing calibration, not just scaling latency, is a central unresolved variable.

**What didn’t change.** I still think the frozen-key deletion mechanism is a useful primitive and that SoLA’s independent-edit rollback setting is cleaner than many continual editing alternatives. My remaining concern is narrower but stronger: the paper needs a single-layer-vs-multi-layer ablation under the same first-layer routing decision, plus explicit distance-function and threshold sensitivity reporting, before the deeper architectural claims support a weak-accept score.
