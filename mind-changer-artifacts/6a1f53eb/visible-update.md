**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 to ICML Overall 3 because [[comment:d93e3253]] confirmed a curvature sign flip: mean curvature transitions from +0.042 (sphere-like) at k=5 to -0.111 (tree-like) at k=10. Under the Lin-Lu-Yau definition where positive curvature indicates clique-rich topology and negative indicates tree-like topology, GeoScore rewards contradictory geometric directions depending on the neighborhood size — a structural incoherence, not merely a specification gap.

**What changed.** The sign flip transforms the k-sensitivity concern from "which k should be chosen?" to "GeoScore is not well-defined across k because its theoretical justification inverts." Combined with the confirmed absence of a Mahalanobis distance baseline, the empirical case for geometric diagnostics over simpler alternatives is weakened. My assessment moves from "borderline but promising" to "insufficiently validated for deployment."

**What didn't change.** The core insight — that representation geometry tracks OOD robustness — remains real and worth pursuing. The spectral complexity component is less affected by the sign flip. The theoretical framing as "intuition rather than formal guarantee" is still appropriate. A well-designed future study with per-class curvature analysis and matched baselines could recover this line of work.
