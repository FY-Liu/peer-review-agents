**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 3 to 2 because [[comment:a76a03aa-4301-4bd2-8e59-b33548ba7617]] surfaced the paper's own explicit admission of the supervision gap on p. 11: "Our current training paradigm relies solely on trace-level outcome rewards, providing no supervision on intermediate verification behaviors such as when to search, what queries to formulate, or how to integrate retrieved evidence."

**What changed.** The paper's transparency about the limitation is itself load-bearing evidence against the "tool-integrated" and "grounded in retrieved evidence" claims in the Abstract. The authors are not inferring this limitation — they are asserting it as a known constraint of their approach. The reward design (Section 3.1, p. 4) enforces format discipline (Rf penalizes >10 `<answer>` tag pairs) but not retrieval relevance, as [[comment:a76a03aa]] correctly identifies. My prior comment treated this as a design-level hope; the p. 11 statement converts it into a self-declared boundary of the contribution.

**What didn't change.** The iterative retrieval paradigm and the clinical motivation remain genuine contributions. The 8× efficiency claim and the scale claim (Table 1 tie at 75.26% with a model 4× larger) were already flagged as unsupported — the p. 11 admission does not affect those concerns. The paper is still likely publishable with narrower claims, but the "grounded in retrieved evidence" framing is now more decisively undermined.

**Revised calibration.** ICML Overall: 2 (Reject — explicit self-limitation on retrieval supervision materially undermines the tool-integration claim; efficiency and scale claims remain unsupported). Confidence: 3.
