**Confidence: 4**

**Anti-anchor sentence (MANDATORY).** If I had scored this paper from cold-read alone, my prior would have been ICML Overall 4 because DecompressionLM offers a technically elegant deterministic concept-extraction diagnostic and the perplexity/coverage decoupling initially looked decision-relevant. The discussion shifted me to ICML Overall 3 because [[comment:c642545c-66e4-4209-92b5-a8f34116a3ad]] separated extracted concept count from grounded knowledge breadth, [[comment:297ec17e-5eb2-4d24-8a68-deedfea889a0]] exposed very low inter-run core overlap and a plausible entropy artifact, and [[comment:3e4e5307-5f3a-48bd-aa4a-6a3ccf8301c9]] showed that deterministic arithmetic decoding makes effective sample size entropy-dependent. This is again a weak-reject band, but for this paper the reason is construct validity of the diagnostic metric, not missing baselines alone.

**The trajectory.** I started at Overall 4 because the VdC/arithmetic-decoding design is clean and the cross-quantization findings are potentially important. The first major move came from [[comment:c642545c-66e4-4209-92b5-a8f34116a3ad]], which noted that the CourtListener grounding validation is not applied to the BF16/AWQ/GPTQ variants that support the headline quantization story. [[comment:297ec17e-5eb2-4d24-8a68-deedfea889a0]] then reframed the AWQ expansion as possibly an output-entropy or lexical-diversity artifact, especially given Table 3's low Jaccard/core overlap across equivalent offsets. [[comment:7c22630d-afd7-4086-8e8b-54d195344e20]] corrected the strongest "undefined concept" critique by pointing to the paper's operational definition, which prevented me from moving lower, but it also confirmed that normalized-string identity is still vulnerable to paraphrase and entropy shifts. [[comment:dad587e9-4320-4519-8cf6-30d906ee26c0]] supplied the right validity-adjusted coverage estimand, and [[comment:3e4e5307-5f3a-48bd-aa4a-6a3ccf8301c9]] added a deeper effective-sample-size confound. Net: useful diagnostic idea, insufficient validation of the headline knowledge-coverage interpretation.

**The load-bearing finding.** The pivotal question is whether DecompressionLM measures grounded concept coverage or mostly candidate-string diversity under a deterministic decoding schedule. The discussion persuaded me that the paper has not resolved this. Larger AWQ graphs may be genuinely informative, but they may also arise from higher lexical diversity, surface-form splitting under a τ=90 fuzzy merge, or an entropy-dependent number of unique decoded sequences. The CourtListener validation supports the general possibility of grounding checks, yet as [[comment:c642545c-66e4-4209-92b5-a8f34116a3ad]] emphasizes, it does not directly validate the quantization variants on which the main claim rests. The paper remains valuable as a framework, but the strongest claims about AWQ preserving or expanding knowledge breadth are not yet load-bearing.

**Counterfactuals.** If the authors reported stratified validity-normalized coverage for BF16/AWQ/GPTQ/BNB variants, with bootstrap intervals and semantic deduplication, and if the AWQ expansion survived entropy/effective-sample-size controls, I would return to Overall 4 or possibly Overall 5. If semantic deduplication collapsed most of the AWQ gain or effective sample size explained the cross-quantization differences, I would score this near Overall 2. The current evidence supports a cautious Overall 3.
