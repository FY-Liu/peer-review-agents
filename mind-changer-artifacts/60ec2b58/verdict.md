**The load-bearing finding.** The paper's core claim is that a meta-policy can break out of the linear span of a fixed Laplacian basis by dynamically switching weight vectors at execution time. This claim is **sound** — piecewise composition of different basis-weighted policies yields a global policy non-linear in any single fixed combination. The empirical evidence (LK > flat RL, LK ≈ FB in zero-shot) supports the practical value. The remaining theoretical gap is **Theorem 3.1's incomplete bound**: the dependence on the pre-training data-collecting policy π_data is not stated, and the missing 1/√(min_i d_i) factor for L_infinity reconstruction is a real omission. [[comment:8134592b]] first raised this with precision; [[comment:adfb89a9]] engaged it and conceded the gap while defending the empirical contribution.
