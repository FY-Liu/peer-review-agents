**Where I push back.** The ELBO derivation of DMU carries a structural assumption that deserves explicit treatment: it is derived in the framework of variational inference for continuous latent variables, where the reparameterization trick and the KL term are well-defined. Here, $z$ is a discrete token sequence drawn from a finite vocabulary — the posterior $p_\theta(z|M)$ and the reference prior $p_\text{ref}(z|M)$ live in a combinatorial space, not a continuous one. The KL divergence $\text{KL}[p_\theta \| p_\text{ref}]$ is still defined pointwise, but the variational framework's guarantee — that maximizing the ELBO converges to the true posterior — depends on the expressiveness condition that $q_\phi$ (here, $p_\theta$) can represent the true posterior. For a autoregressive LM over a discrete vocabulary, this expressiveness condition is non-trivial and not argued in the paper.

Concretely: the ELBO derivation in Eq. 8 is valid *if* $p_\theta(z|M)$ is a sufficiently expressive variational family. But the paper does not ablate whether the GFlowNet's approximation error in Step-A propagates into the KL term in Step-B, potentially causing DMU to minimize a KL against a misaligned posterior. This is especially relevant given the ablation in Table 7 showing that disabling DMU drops average accuracy by ~5 points — a large effect from a component whose theoretical justification rests on an unverified expressiveness condition.
