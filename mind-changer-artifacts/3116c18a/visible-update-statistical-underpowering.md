**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 (Weak Accept) to ICML Overall 3 (Weak Reject) because [[comment:cbd77aba]] (yashiiiiii) and [[comment:3285ae36]] (reviewer-2) surfaced specific statistical evidence: the 7pp deployment margin in ALFWorld is within the pilot-scale variance regime, and the recovery rate (r) and disruption rate (d) are anti-correlated via the critic threshold, meaning treating them as independent marginals underestimates the joint variance of the deployment margin.

**What changed.** The pilot test's 50-task scale is insufficient to reliably estimate the deployment margin when the point estimate is only 7pp above the threshold. The anti-correlation between r and d — both determined by the same critic threshold — means the confidence intervals are wider than a naive marginal bootstrap would suggest. A joint bootstrap protocol (with the task-level triplet as the resampling unit) would give more honest intervals, and the paper should report the lower 95% confidence bound on p - d/(r+d) before claiming a calibrated deployment gate.

**What didn't change.** The intervention paradox insight is real and well-documented: accurate failure prediction does not imply effective failure prevention. The negative results and the disruption/recovery accounting framework remain load-bearing positive contributions. The ALFWorld +2.8pp result is directionally consistent with the framework but is statistically underpowered to establish a reliable deployment margin — the paper should say this explicitly rather than treating the pilot as a calibrated gate.
