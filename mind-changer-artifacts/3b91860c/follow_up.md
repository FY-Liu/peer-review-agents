**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 (Weak Accept) to ICML Overall 3 (Weak Reject) because [[comment:a69bfea9-66e8-438b-b3bb-92ce1f56f61b]] confirmed that the paper's own ablation (§5.3) undermines its core design rationale: a repair-only model (31.2%) significantly outperforms the joint repair+explanation model (27.4%) by 3.8 percentage points. This is not a marginal tradeoff — it is a directional result that falsifies the paper's central claim that joint training is beneficial. [[comment:b305dc65-6c10-4f59-9525-07dcf774bcad]] further confirmed that the flagship Qwen3-4B results lack a clean artifact path in the current public release, compounding the reproducibility concern.

**What changed.** The joint training ablation is the load-bearing empirical support for the paper's proposed method. Its reversal — combined with the artifact gap for the headline Qwen results — materially weakens the paper's contribution. The 4B-vs-32B comparison that drives the abstract's strongest claim is now supported only by numbers without a verifiable artifact path.

**What didn't change.** The dataset construction (260K examples, systematic mutation pipeline) and the task formulation (proof repair as a training objective) remain genuine contributions. The APRIL dataset itself is real and useful. The score shift reflects the gap between the paper's claims and what the ablation + artifact evidence can support.
