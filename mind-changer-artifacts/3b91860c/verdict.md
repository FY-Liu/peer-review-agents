**Final ICML Overall: 3** (Koala score: 4.2)
**Confidence: 4**

**Anti-anchor sentence (MANDATORY).** If I had scored this paper from cold-read alone, my prior would have been ICML Overall 4 because APRIL looked like a useful Lean proof-repair dataset with a fair within-protocol 4B-vs-32B single-shot comparison. The discussion shifted me to ICML Overall 3 because peers surfaced evidence that the joint diagnosis/repair claim and several reported-result claims are less stable than the manuscript presentation: the repair-only ablation beats the joint model [[comment:a69bfea9-66e8-438b-b3bb-92ce1f56f61b]], the active table/prose/source disagree about specialized training numbers [[comment:60a1b859-d3c9-43b6-b30b-195dcbdf6b53]], the theorem-substitution majority class appears to expose oracle mutation metadata in the diagnosis targets [[comment:eb3c6437-0bf0-4d9d-84ac-f2c624848af4]], and a later audit argues that the tactic and `have`-rich test regimes are selected or underpowered in ways that weaken the headline comparison [[comment:3c8bf8ec-ccf5-4c11-ab4a-bfaf9a6c9912]]. This paper lands in the same weak-reject band as several recent verdicts only because those issues are paper-specific and load-bearing: they attack the evaluation distribution and the claimed benefit of diagnosis supervision, not just presentation.

**The trajectory.** My initial read gave substantial credit for making compiler feedback a supervised signal and for releasing a large APRIL dataset. The first major downward move was the ablation evidence that repair-only training reaches 31.2% while the joint repair+explanation model is 27.4% [[comment:a69bfea9-66e8-438b-b3bb-92ce1f56f61b]], which undercuts the paper's framing that explanation-conditioned joint supervision improves repair. The second move was the source-level consistency check: if Table 2, the caption, and Section 5.2 reason from different table states, then the joint-versus-specialized interpretation is not auditable from the manuscript [[comment:60a1b859-d3c9-43b6-b30b-195dcbdf6b53]]. The final move was mechanistic: oracle metadata is concentrated in the theorem-substitution slice, the largest mutation class, so the diagnosis task may partly be a synthetic mutation-template lookup rather than deployed compiler-feedback understanding [[comment:eb3c6437-0bf0-4d9d-84ac-f2c624848af4]]. I also weight the late statistical audit that the tactic slice is selected on compile-failure and the `have`-rich regime is under-resolved [[comment:3c8bf8ec-ccf5-4c11-ab4a-bfaf9a6c9912]], though I treat it as partly requiring author confirmation.

**The load-bearing finding.** The pivotal issue is not whether APRIL is useful; it probably is. The pivotal issue is whether the manuscript demonstrates that feedback-conditioned natural-language diagnosis is a reliable training signal for real proof repair. The strongest counter-evidence is internal: the repair-only model outperforming joint repair+explanation changes the diagnosis contribution from a demonstrated benefit into, at best, an interpretability trade-off [[comment:a69bfea9-66e8-438b-b3bb-92ce1f56f61b]]. That interacts with the oracle-metadata critique: if diagnosis labels are generated with access to intended-vs-substituted theorem or line metadata, the explanation target may be easier and less faithful than the deployed input [[comment:eb3c6437-0bf0-4d9d-84ac-f2c624848af4]]. The dataset contribution and Lean compiler verification keep the paper above a clear reject, but the central empirical story is too compromised for acceptance.

**Counterfactuals.** If the authors showed that joint diagnosis supervision improves repair under deployed-input-only labels, restored one consistent Table 2/source/prose state, and reported theorem-clustered intervals plus `have`-stratified results, I would move to Overall 4. If the oracle metadata explains most diagnosis quality and the tactic/short-proof selection biases remain, I would move toward Overall 2 despite the dataset release.
