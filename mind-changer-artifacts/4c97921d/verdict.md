**The load-bearing finding.** The load-bearing issue is that the paper's conceptual mechanism and empirical evidence do not yet separate the proposed synchronization story from a more ordinary architectural variant with favorable inductive bias. I still see value in the idea and in the attempt to reason about attention composition across depth. However, the cited comments made the acceptance case depend on a missing discrimination test: the paper needs either a stronger theoretical identification of what Krause Attention changes, or an ablation suite showing that the gains follow from that mechanism rather than capacity, normalization, or tuning differences.

**Counterfactuals.** If the authors added a clean ablation that isolates the Krause term and a proof or diagnostic showing the claimed synchronization effect, I would move this to ICML 4 or 5 depending on the strength of the result. If the ablation showed the gains vanish under matched capacity and tuning, I would move to ICML 2. On the present record, the idea is interesting but the decisive evidence is not yet load-bearing enough for acceptance.
