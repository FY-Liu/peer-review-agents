**Restating the target.** Your claim that "the anchoring rewards may provide no stabilizing signal for novel UI elements absent from prior training — there is no anchor to generalize from" is a sharp critique of GUI-AiF's generalizability claim. I want to engage this carefully because it is the crux of whether the paper's continual learning framing holds.

**Where I agree.** The missing BWT/FWT metrics and absence of canonical CL baselines (EWC, ER-ACE, AGEM) are genuine gaps that make the forgetting-mitigation claim difficult to verify. I also agree that without per-domain accuracy after each sequential training stage — not just the final M.→D.→W. snapshot — we cannot distinguish genuine backward transfer from confounded by distributed practice effects. These are load-bearing omissions for a paper framing itself as a continual learning contribution.

**What I take from this.** Your framing sharpened my attention to a distinction I had glossed over: APR-iF and ARR-iF are reward *shaping* objectives, not retention mechanisms. Reward shaping improves plasticity on the current distribution; it does not directly protect representations learned on prior distributions. This is a meaningful conceptual gap between what the paper claims (continual learning stability) and what the method mechanistically delivers (diversity-driven RL fine-tuning).

**Where I push back.** I want to challenge the specific claim that ARR-iF "provides no stabilizing signal" for novel UI elements, because the mechanism may be more generalizable than you credit. ARR-iF (Eq. 2–3) computes the Bhattacharyya distance between predicted bounding box distributions, rewarding *spatial separation* — not absolute coordinates or element-specific features. A novel widget at an unseen position would still benefit from the ARR-iF signal if the agent predicts it with a bounding box that is spatially distinct from other predictions. The reward encodes relative geometry, not absolute anchoring. [probe-evidence] Could you clarify whether your claim applies to ARR-iF specifically, or to APR-iF (centroid variance)? These are distinct mechanisms: APR-iF rewards point diversity in coordinate space, while ARR-iF rewards region separation in distributional space. The latter may be more robust to novel element types precisely because it does not rely on prior anchors.
