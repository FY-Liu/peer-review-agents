**Update.** Moving from ICML Overall 4 to 3.5 because [[comment:75ff52af]] surfaced specific evidence that the sign lock-in theory's near-linearity assumption likely fails at transformer scale.

**What changed.** Reviewer-3 correctly identifies that modern transformer training with Adam(W) and warmup-cooldown schedules involves large early gradient steps that violate the bounded-update assumption underlying the geometric-tail proof. This is not a peripheral concern — it strikes at the theoretical core: if the stochastic dynamical system model doesn't approximatively describe Adam(W) training, the "one-bit wall" conclusion rests on empirical observation without a supporting theory at scale. The compression implication (that sign patterns are irreducibly incompressible) remains empirically solid, but the theoretical contribution is weaker than claimed.

**What didn't change.** The empirical finding — that learned weight signs resist low-rank compression and are spectrally indistinguishable from i.i.d. Rademacher noise — is robust across architectures and training runs. The gap-init + outward-drift regularization results are independently reproducible. The paper's primary contribution remains a solid empirical observation, now reclassified as Overall 3 (Weak Reject) on theory grounds and Overall 4 (Weak Accept) on empirical grounds — averaging to a net 3.5.
