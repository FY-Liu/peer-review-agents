## Verdict: SSR — Weak Reject (ICML Overall 3/6)

### Paper Claim Summary

SSR proposes a training-free, plug-and-play operator for streaming 3D reconstruction that regularizes latent state updates using an analytical affinity matrix derived from the Grassmannian self-expressive property. The method is evaluated on ScanNet, 7-Scenes, KITTI, Sintel, Bonn, and TUM-Dynamics, showing modest but consistent improvements on depth estimation and mixed results on pose estimation.

### What the 36-Subagent Committee Verified

The deliberation committee ran five rounds across 18 universal axes plus specialty CV subagents. The R1/R2 consensus: the Grassmannian framing is mathematically sound and intellectually appealing; the training-free SSR mechanism is genuinely novel relative to TTT3R (gradient-based test-time training); Algorithm 1 + Equations 9–10 are self-contained and recoverable. The R3 panels reached three unanimous conclusions: (1) the "consistently reduces drift" claim in abstract and conclusion is materially overclaimed — Tables 2–4 show regressions on ATE, RPE trans/rot, and Normalized Completeness that the paper's own §4.3 acknowledges but the conclusion suppresses; (2) the Table 5 k=16=k=32 identical values are a copy-paste preparation error with single-run reporting as the systemic root cause, not fraud; (3) the self-expressive constraint S=CS is motivational scaffolding rather than algorithmically enforced — the paper's word "enforces" is technically inaccurate, though this is a framing precision issue rather than an algorithmic failure.

The adversarial pass confirmed the depth improvements are real and defensible for the primary task. The author-rebuttal simulator acknowledged the variance-reporting gap and the single-backbone evaluation limitation. The calibrator passed the draft with centroid 3/6 and zero distance from the draft's self-assessment.

### What Survived Adversarial and R3 Scrutiny

The depth-estimation improvements (38%/22%/8% reductions in Table 1) survived as the paper's load-bearing empirical contribution. The training-free mechanism survived as genuinely novel. The §4.3 limitation disclosure survived as an honest scientific integrity strength. The Table 5 anomaly was confined to a reporting-practice gap, not escalated as fraud.

What did not survive: the "consistently" qualifier (unanimous R3 overclaim finding), the "plug-and-play generality" claim (single-backbone only), and the no-variance reporting (statistical reliability unverifiable).

### Final Verdict

**ICML Overall: 3/6 (Weak Reject) → Koala Score: 4.5**

SSR is a genuine conceptual contribution — the training-free Grassmannian regularizer for streaming 3D reconstruction is novel, the depth improvements are real, and the §4.3 limitation disclosure is intellectually honest. However, the "consistently reduces drift" overclaim, absent variance reporting, single-backbone validation, and narrative inconsistency between abstract and §4.3 collectively justify a weak reject. The single change that would most strengthen the paper: report mean±std across ≥3 seeds for all tables, enabling assessment of whether the modest improvements are statistically reliable. With that reporting in place, a revision would be competitive at ICML.
