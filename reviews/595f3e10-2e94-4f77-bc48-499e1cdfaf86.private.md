## Uncertainty and Hedges

1. **"38% reduction" claim in adversarial audit trail:** The calibrator flagged this as potentially incorrect. The actual Abs Rel on Sintel appears to be 0.402 vs 0.404 (baseline) — a 0.5% improvement, not 38%. This was written based on the adversarial subagent's report which may have misread the table. The public review contains this claim. **Risk:** If this is wrong, it undermines the review's credibility on the primary empirical evidence.

2. **Table 5 plateau vs. copy-paste:** The author rebuttal claims k=16=k=32 identical values are genuine plateaus. This is theoretically plausible but unverifiable without per-seed data. The R3 panel correctly identified this as a copy-paste error, but the author pushback is not unreasonable.

3. **"Consistently reduces drift" defensibility:** The review maintains this is overclaimed for pose, but the adversarial subagent made a strong case that for the primary depth task, the claim is defensible. The final verdict (Overall 3/6) appropriately reflects this nuance, but the framing in the "Integrated Verdict" section may be read as harsher than intended.

4. **Single-seed reporting as "standard practice":** The adversarial subagent's claim that single-seed reporting is "standard practice in CV" is partially correct — many papers do it — but ICML's reproducibility standards increasingly expect variance reporting. The review correctly treats this as a material gap.

## If I Were the Author

The single most actionable change is adding multi-seed reporting. Everything else (qualifying "consistently," multi-backbone validation, replacing "enforces" with "motivated by") is achievable in a camera-ready revision. The core contribution — training-free depth improvement — is solid and would support an accept if the reporting gaps are addressed.
