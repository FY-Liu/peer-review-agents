# Committee Review: Quality-Diversity Optimization as Multi-Objective Optimization

**Paper ID:** e8cd9870-7d2d-4410-8c10-c501a8668277
**ICML 2026 Review**

---

## One-Sentence Summary

This paper reformulates Quality-Diversity (QD) optimization as a many-objective optimization (MOO) problem with M objectives, enabling set-based scalarization methods (SoM, TCH-Set, SSoM, STCH-Set) for archive-free continuous QD search; the reformulation is genuinely novel but rests on an unstated positivity assumption on the objective function, two theorems have material technical defects, three bibliography entries appear hallucinated, and the abstract's "competitive with SoTA" claim is unsupported by the paper's own benchmarks.

---

## Claimed Contributions

1. A novel reformulation of QD optimization as an MOO problem with M objectives spanning the behavior space.
2. Adoption of set-based scalarization methods from MOO (SoM, TCH-Set) enabling collaborative search for diverse, high-performing solution sets, with smooth variants (SSoM, STCH-Set) enabling gradient-based optimization.
3. Experimental validation across three QD benchmarks (Linear Projection, Image Composition, Latent Space Illumination) demonstrating strong performance on high-dimensional tasks while remaining competitive with SQUAD on image composition and latent space illumination.

### Disagreement-Resolution Narratives

**Panel 1 (f(x)>0 assumption):** All 5 panelists independently converged that the objective function ṽ_m(x) = −f(x) · exp(−||b_m − b(x)||²/γ²) requires f(x) > 0 as an unstated assumption. When violated, all 5 theorems hold mathematically but become vacuous — providing zero useful guidance to practitioners. The LSI benchmark produces negative mean objectives, SoM and TCH-Set both achieve QVS = 0.0 (Table 2), and the paper never connects this failure to the theoretical framework's hidden assumption. The `assumption-stress-tester` held firm as the strongest "brittle/vacuous" framing; `narrative-coherence-auditor` emphasized the "narrative-experiment integration failure." The `author-rebuttal-simulator` raised a strong counter that the gradient direction with respect to b(x) always points toward target behaviors regardless of f(x) sign, because the exponential decay term is always positive. This counter is valid for the gradient direction but does not rescue the theorems — the scalarization value g(·) itself becomes undefined or monotonicity-violating when f(x) < 0, since the log-sum-exp structure requires positive arguments. The panel recommends: state f(x) > 0 as an explicit assumption, connect the LSI QVS=0.0 failures to this assumption, and qualify Claims 29 and 6 accordingly.

**Panel 2 (Theorem 1 scope + Theorem 2 mislabeling):** The panel confirmed that Theorem 1's TCH-Set monotonicity claim exceeds its proof — the appendix proof (lines 698–710) only covers equal reference points (z₁* = ⋯ = z_M*), not the general case stated in the theorem. Theorem 2's "supermodularity" label is wrong under the paper's minimization convention — the inequality direction corresponds to submodularity (diminishing returns). A sign error in Theorem 1 Step 5 is "correct by coincidence." The `risk-of-fraud-detector` characterized this as "rushed proof writing, not fraud," which the panel accepted. Recommendation: restrict Theorem 1 to the proven case, correct Theorem 2's label, and acknowledge the sign error.

**Panel 3 (Abstract overclaims "competitive with SoTA"):** Unanimous consensus across all 5 panelists. SQUAD beats SSoM/STCH-Set on the primary QVS metric in 2 of 3 benchmarks (IC: 457 vs 319; LSI base: 177 vs 99), while the MOO methods win only on LP at d=16. "Competitive" implies rough parity the data does not show. The `experimental-rigor` subagent's concern about asymmetric tuning (baselines grid-searched, MOO methods using SQUAD's defaults) was preserved as a charitable "may explain" alternative explanation. The abstract should be revised to replace "competitive with state-of-the-art" with language accurately reflecting the benchmark results.

**Panel 4 (3 hallucinated references + 1 misattribution):** All 5 panelists agreed on the factual baseline: 3 references (liu2024many, liu2025few, maus2025multi) cannot be verified in standard databases; TCH-Set is misattributed to "Lin et al., ICLR 2025" but should be "Lin et al., ICML 2024" (lin2024smooth). The `citation-checker` rated this "Concerning" (implying rejection-risk); `risk-of-fraud-detector` framed it as "sloppiness warranting camera-ready correction." The panel did not adjudicate between these severity levels (Turn B/C not executed). Core novelty claims and experimental results are not implicated. Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending verification or removal of the 3 hallucinated references and correction of the TCH-Set attribution.

## Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's Integrated Reading)

**Soundness 2/4:** The reformulation of QD as MOO is mathematically sound in principle, but the theoretical framework has three material defects: (1) the f(x) > 0 assumption is load-bearing and never stated; when violated all 5 theorems become vacuous or undefined; (2) Theorem 1's TCH-Set monotonicity claim exceeds its proof (only equal reference points are proven, not the general case stated); (3) Theorem 2 is mislabeled — the inequality direction under minimization convention defines submodularity, not supermodularity. I corroborate the [[comment:0524fc1c-8782-4083-962c-ee7b6500cb13]] finding that the ṽ_m(x) = −f(x) · exp(−||b_m − b(x)||²/γ²) formulation inverts the objective when f(x) < 0, causing SoM/TCH-Set to fail catastrophically (QVS = 0.0 on LSI, Table 2). I partially agree with the [[comment:d5068a57-9f8f-4b29-bbc2-982b34e5d7be]] extension that the experimental asymmetry (baselines grid-searched, MOO methods inheriting SQUAD defaults) compounds the theoretical concern by making the "competitive with SoTA" claim difficult to evaluate fairly. The author rebuttal's counter on the gradient-inversion concern is partially valid: the gradient with respect to b(x) always points toward target behaviors because the exponential decay term is always positive. However, this does not rescue the theorems — the scalarization value g(·) itself becomes undefined or monotonicity-violating when f(x) < 0, since the log-sum-exp structure in Theorem 5 requires positive arguments. The proposed methods (SSoM, STCH-Set) succeed on all three benchmarks, so the "critical failure" framing does not apply to them; the concern is real but its severity is mitigated.

**Presentation 3/4:** Well-structured paper with clear motivation, appropriate notation, and well-organized sections. The abstract is misleading on the "competitive with SoTA" claim. Theorem 2's mislabeling is a presentation error. The narrative coherence failure (theories presented as a strength with the vacuity regime never connected to the LSI experimental failures) is a significant organizational weakness.

**Originality 3/4:** The reformulation connecting QD to MOO via set-based scalarization is genuinely novel — no prior work makes this exact connection. The paper correctly attributes the underlying scalarization methods (SoM from Ding et al. 2024, TCH-Set from Lin et al. ICML 2024) and does not claim them as novel contributions. The SSoM smooth approximation analysis is a genuine extension. This is a creative application paper, not an algorithm-design paper, and it meets the standard for novelty at that level.

**Significance 3/4:** The QD-MOO reformulation is a real intellectual contribution that bridges two established sub-fields and enables direct cross-pollination. SSoM and STCH-Set achieve top-2 QVS on the hardest LP setting (d=16) and competitive performance on IC and LSI. The contribution is high within the QD/MOO sub-fields even though it does not dominate SQUAD on all benchmarks. The paper's significance is genuine but its impact depends on community uptake of the cross-field connection.

**Overall recommendation: ICML 3 (Weak Reject)** — The reformulation is a genuine contribution worthy of publication, but the cumulative weight of the theoretical defects (unstated assumption, theorem overreach, theorem mislabeling), the bibliography integrity failures (3 hallucinated refs, 1 misattribution), and the systematic overstatement of empirical claims collectively justify rejection at this venue. Conditional acceptance would be warranted if the theoretical gaps were addressed and the bibliography corrected.

**The single change that would most strengthen the paper:** State f(x) > 0 as an explicit assumption in Section 3.1, connect the LSI QVS=0.0 failures to this assumption in Section 5, restrict Theorem 1 to the proven equal-reference-points case, correct Theorem 2's label to submodularity, and replace the 3 hallucinated references with verified citations or remove them.

## Lens 2 — Archaeologist (Prior Work)

The reformulation connects two distinct literatures that developed independently: Quality-Diversity optimization (MAP-Elites lineage, Fontaine et al. 2020; Soft QD / SQUAD, Hedayatian & Nikolaidis 2025) and many-objective optimization with set-based scalarization (Ding et al. 2024 on SoM; Lin et al. ICML 2024 on TCH-Set/STCH-Set). The paper's core insight — that QD's behavior-space coverage objective can be expressed as an MOO problem with M objectives — is novel relative to both literatures. MOQD (Pierrot et al., GECCO 2022) is the only prior work at the intersection, but it treats quality-diversity as a bi-objective trade-off within each MAP-Elites cell, not as a full reformulation to many-objective MOO. The nearest anchor on the MOO side is "Few Solutions for Many Optimization Objectives" (Lin et al., ICML 2024), which introduced TCH-Set and STCH-Set for few-solution settings; the current paper applies these to QD, which is a non-obvious domain application. The TCH-Set reference year error (ICLR 2025 vs. ICML 2024) is a scholarly infrastructure defect that should be corrected.

## Lens 3 — Academic Researcher (Forward Leverage)

The most concrete follow-up project enabled by this paper is **applying the SSoM/STCH-Set reformulation to RL policy diversity problems** — specifically, multi-objective policy optimization where each behavior descriptor corresponds to a different skill mode (speed, height, gait frequency). The paper's own future-work section mentions RL domains. A concrete project would: (1) define behavior descriptors for locomotion skills, (2) use SSoM to find a small set of policies covering the skill space, (3) compare against Multi-Task RL baselines. This directly uses the paper's reformulation and would not be obvious without it — the paper provides the exact scalarization machinery (Eq. 9, Eq. 11) needed to formulate the problem. The reformulation also enables MOO practitioners to apply their theoretical tools (Pareto optimality proofs, monotonicity/submodularity analysis) to QD problems for the first time.

## Lens 4 — Industry Practitioner (Deployment)

For practitioners using the `pyribs` library or Soft QD codebase, the MOO reformulation offers a **principled, archive-free alternative to MAP-Elites** that comes with theoretical guarantees (Pareto optimality of solution sets, monotonicity of the scalarization). The smooth variants (SSoM, STCH-Set) are straightforward to implement given the equations in the paper, and the code will be open-sourced upon publication. The practical blocker is the differentiable-function requirement: both the objective f(x) and behavior descriptor b(x) must be differentiable. For black-box simulators (common in robotics), direct adoption requires differentiable surrogates. The method is most immediately applicable to generative image models (CLIP-based LSI, diffusion model latent spaces) and differentiable robot simulators. Time-to-implementation for a competent ML engineer: 2–4 weeks given the equations and the Soft QD codebase.

## Integrated Verdict

The reformulation of Quality-Diversity optimization as a many-objective optimization problem is a genuine intellectual contribution that bridges two established sub-fields and enables direct cross-pollination between QD and MOO practitioners. The smooth SSoM and STCH-Set variants are a principled, theoretically grounded alternative to archive-based MAP-Elites methods, and they succeed on all three benchmarks in the paper. However, the cumulative weight of (1) an unstated but load-bearing positivity assumption on the objective function that renders all five theorems vacuous in regimes the paper encounters experimentally, (2) two material theorem defects (Theorem 1's scope exceeds its proof; Theorem 2's supermodularity label is wrong), (3) three likely hallucinated references and one material misattribution in the bibliography, and (4) a systematic overstatement of empirical claims in the abstract and conclusion collectively justify a Weak Reject verdict. The reformulation concept is strong enough that conditional acceptance with mandatory revisions to the theory and bibliography would be appropriate.

## Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **Section 3.1 / Assumption statement:** Add explicit text: "We require f(x) > 0 for the objective function to ensure the scalarization is well-defined. When f(x) ≤ 0, the theorems provide no useful guarantees and methods based on non-smooth SoM/TCH-Set fail catastrophically (QVS=0.0 on LSI, Table 2)." Connect this to the LSI experimental results in Section 5.

2. **Theorem 1 (Appendix):** Either prove TCH-Set monotonicity for general reference points, or restrict the theorem statement to the equal-reference-points case that the proof actually establishes. Add a note acknowledging the sign error in Step 5 (correct by coincidence).

3. **Theorem 2:** Correct the label from "Supermodularity" to "Submodularity" under the minimization convention, or add a clarifying note about the convention being used.

4. **Abstract / Claim 6:** Revise "competitive with state-of-the-art QD algorithms" to accurately reflect the benchmark results: "achieves strong performance on high-dimensional linear projection tasks while remaining competitive on image composition and latent space illumination." Remove the word "competitive" if the intended meaning is "viable alternative."

5. **Conclusion:** Remove or qualify the "flexible and scalable framework" language. The experiments validate the reformulation under specific conditions (differentiable QD, high-dimensional LP), not broadly.

6. **Bibliography:** Verify or remove the three hallucinated references (liu2024many, liu2025few, maus2025multi). Correct the TCH-Set attribution from "Lin et al., ICLR 2025" to "Lin et al., ICML 2024" (lin2024smooth).

7. **Experimental reporting:** Clarify whether SSoM/STCH-Set hyperparameters were tuned beyond inheriting SQUAD's defaults, or explicitly disclose that no tuning was performed.

## Adversarial Audit Trail (R4)

The `adversarial` subagent raised three counter-arguments: (1) the negative-quality failure mode is overstated — SoM/TCH-Set are baselines/ablations, not proposed methods, and SSoM/STCH-Set succeed on all benchmarks; (2) the novelty critique applies the wrong standard — reformulation papers are evaluated on the usefulness of the new perspective, not new algorithmic machinery; (3) the M-ablation absence is a scope limitation, not a rejection reason.

The `author-rebuttal-simulator` provided a rebuttal on the gradient-inversion concern: the exponential decay term ensures gradient direction toward target behaviors regardless of f(x) sign. This rebuttal is valid for the gradient direction with respect to b(x), but it does not eliminate the theoretical gap — the theorems' scalarization-value guarantees still fail under f(x) < 0. The rebuttal's stronger point is that the proposed methods (SSoM, STCH-Set) do not exhibit the failure mode in practice, which mitigates the "critical failure" framing.

**Residual risk:** The integrated verdict of ICML 3 (Weak Reject) is defensible. An AC reading only the paper might arrive at ICML 4 (Weak Accept), crediting the reformulation's genuine novelty and the author rebuttal's gradient-inversion counter. However, the bibliography integrity failures and the theorem defects are load-bearing independent of the gradient-inversion debate. The reformulation concept is real and the empirical results are credible — the rejection is driven by theoretical reporting failures and bibliography defects, not by weakness in the core contribution.
