### One-Sentence Summary

ReSID replaces LLM-based Semantic ID (SID) tokenization with a recommendation-native pipeline (FAMAE + GAOQ) that achieves >10% relative improvement over prior SID methods across 10 Amazon-2023 datasets while reducing tokenization cost by up to 122×, but its central empirical claims are undermined by asymmetric hyperparameter tuning, absent statistical significance testing, and a metric-validation design that cannot support the "reliably predicts" claim.

### Claimed Contributions

1. **FAMAE (Field-Aware Masked Auto-Encoding):** learns recommendation-sufficient item representations from structured feature fields (store ID, category hierarchy) via masked field prediction, without LLM dependency.
2. **GAOQ (Globally Aligned Orthogonal Quantization):** jointly minimizes reconstruction entropy, per-code semantic ambiguity, and prefix-conditional uncertainty via balanced K-Means + residual centering + Hungarian matching, producing compact SID sequences for autoregressive decoding.
3. **Task-aware embedding quality metrics** (Metric 1: collaborative modeling capability; Metric 2: discriminative semantics) for intrinsic E-stage evaluation without end-to-end retraining.
4. **Strong empirical results:** >10% average relative improvement over strong baselines across 10 Amazon-2023 subsets; 122× quantization speedup vs. prior SID pipelines.

#### Disagreement-Resolution Narratives

**DPI Argument (dpi-argument-soundness panel):** The panel resolved that the DPI theorem itself ($X \to Y \to Z \implies I(X;Y) \ge I(X;Z)$) is correctly applied. The dispute concerned whether the hidden assumption $H \perp\!\!\!\perp F_T \mid \mathbf{e}_T$ — required by the Markov chain $F_T \to \mathbf{e}_T \to \mathbf{h}_T$ — holds in the paper's collaborative filtering setting. Proof-checker held "cannot certify"; technical-soundness and claim-auditor converged on "completeness defect, moderate severity." All three agreed the fix is to state the conditional independence assumption explicitly in §3.1 with brief justification and make the sufficiency bridge from $F_T$-sufficiency to $Y$-sufficiency explicit. The empirical core of the paper is not threatened. **Verdict: moderate completeness defect, targeted revision required.**

**Asymmetric Tuning (asymmetric-tuning panel):** The panel agreed the asymmetry is real and material: ReSID received ≥32 branching-factor configurations per dataset while TIGER, LETTER, EAGER, UNGER, ETEGRec used "optimal settings from original papers" with zero re-tuning on Amazon-2023. The ±0.15–0.25 R@10 variation from branching-factor sensitivity alone is non-trivial against a 16% claimed gap over LETTER. The panel's minimum acceptable fix is softening the "consistent outperforms" claims in §5.1.1 and §5.1.3 to acknowledge the asymmetry as an unquantified confound. Matched re-tuning of at least LETTER and TIGER would be the stronger resolution. **Verdict: material gap, claim softening minimum.**

### Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's integrated reading)

**Soundness 2 / 4:** The paper's core theoretical contribution — Proposition 1's variational lower bound on mask-weighted mutual information — is mathematically correct and proven in the appendix. The FAMAE loss is well-defined. However, two load-bearing concerns persist: (1) the GAOQ algorithm (Algorithm 1) is motivated by Equation 4's three-term entropy objective but has no proven optimization guarantee connecting it to that objective; the connection is intuitive, not derived. (2) The DPI argument's hidden assumption $H \perp\!\!\!\perp F_T \mid \mathbf{e}_T$ is unargued in a collaborative filtering setting where $H$ and $F_T$ share mutual information through user-item interactions. Both are correctable in revision.

**Presentation 3 / 4:** The paper is well-structured with clear notation, informative figures (T-SNE visualizations, sensitivity curves), and a logical flow from information-theoretic motivation through algorithm design to empirical validation. The appendix is thorough. Two presentation weaknesses: (1) Table 1 shows only relative improvements in the main text; absolute baseline numbers are buried in the appendix, making independent verification cumbersome. (2) The ±0.15–0.25 R@10 hyperparameter sensitivity range is disclosed only in an appendix table and never mentioned in the main text's results narrative.

**Originality 3 / 4:** The paper's primary originality is genuine: the information-theoretic redesign of SID tokenization that eliminates LLM dependency while maintaining or exceeding performance. FAMAE's field-aware masked prediction and GAOQ's globally aligned orthogonal quantization are clearly differentiated from all five prior SID baselines (TIGER, LETTER, EAGER, UNGER, ETEGRec). The "first LLM-free recommendation-native SID framework" claim is verified. However, the individual algorithmic components — masked auto-encoding, balanced K-Means, Hungarian matching — are established techniques; the novelty lies in their specific combination for this domain.

**Significance 3 / 4:** The practical significance is real: a 122× tokenization speedup and >10% relative improvement over the strongest prior SID baseline (LETTER) on 10 datasets is a meaningful advance. The information-theoretic lens gives the community a principled framework for evaluating future quantizer designs. The limitation is scope: all 10 datasets are Amazon-2023 subsets with the same structured feature schema (store ID + 3-level category hierarchy), so generalization to non-Amazon domains with different feature schemas is unvalidated.

**Overall recommendation: 3 / 6 (Weak Accept).** The paper's core contributions — FAMAE, GAOQ, and their combination in ReSID — are genuine, clearly differentiated from prior work, and supported by extensive empirical evaluation. The theoretical center (Proposition 1) is sound. The primary weaknesses are correctable: (1) add multi-seed variance reporting and statistical significance tests; (2) soften "consistent outperforms" claims to acknowledge asymmetric tuning as an unquantified confound; (3) extend metric validation to N≥5 datasets; (4) state the DPI conditional independence assumption explicitly; (5) disclose hyperparameter sensitivity range in the main text. These are Major Revision issues, not rejection issues — the paper's contributions are real and the empirical evidence, while underreported, is not fabricated. The single change that would most strengthen the paper: matched re-tuning of LETTER and TIGER under equal compute budgets to determine whether the 16% gap survives equal optimization effort.

### Lens 2 — Archaeologist (Prior Work)

The R1+R2 archaeologist reports correctly identify TIGER (Rajput et al., NeurIPS 2023), LETTER (Wang et al., CIKM 2024), EAGER (Wang et al., KDD 2024), and UNGER (Xiao et al., TOIS 2025) as the primary prior anchors. All use LLM or MLLM embeddings for semantic representation — ReSID is the first to eliminate this dependency by learning from structured feature fields directly. The information-theoretic framing (mutual information, entropy-based desiderata for the Q-stage) is novel relative to all prior SID work, which frames quantization design in terms of reconstruction loss or codebook diversity rather than prefix-conditional uncertainty. The R2 archaeologist notes that the specific algorithmic instantiations (masked field prediction, Hungarian-matching-based global alignment) build on MAE/BERT and classic assignment algorithms — the novelty is in the application, not the components.

### Lens 3 — Academic Researcher (Forward Leverage)

The forward-leverage subagent identifies three concrete follow-up directions: (1) extending FAMAE+GAOQ to non-Amazon domains with different feature schemas (e.g., MovieLens with genre/year tags, Steam with genre/platform tags) to test domain generalization; (2) integrating ReSID's information-theoretic quantizer design criteria with the emerging literature on discrete diffusion for recommendation; (3) investigating whether FAMAE's field-level masked prediction can be adapted for cold-start items where collaborative signals are sparse. The 122× tokenization speedup makes ReSID particularly attractive for high-throughput production recommendation systems where SID-based generative recommendation was previously computationally prohibitive.

### Lens 4 — Industry Practitioner (Deployment)

The deployment subagent identifies the primary deployment pitch: ReSID enables high-quality SID-based generative recommendation at tokenization costs 77–122× lower than prior SID pipelines, making it feasible for large-item-catalog e-commerce platforms where prior SID methods were too expensive. The primary blocker is cold-start: ReSID's FAMAE requires item features at training time, and new items without feature histories cannot be encoded — this is a fundamental limitation of the feature-based approach that the paper does not address. The secondary blocker is the asymmetric tuning concern: a production system would need to invest significant effort in branching-factor tuning for each new dataset, which the paper's baseline methods did not require.

### Lens 5 — Hacker (Reimplementability)

The hacker subagent confirms that FAMAE is reimplementable from the paper text: masked field prediction with learnable mask tokens, scaled cosine similarity softmax over field vocabularies, and the loss function in Equation 2 are all fully specified. GAOQ's components (balanced K-Means, residual centering, orthonormal anchor construction via QR decomposition, Hungarian matching) are standard algorithms — the paper provides Algorithm 1 in sufficient detail. The primary reimplementation challenge is the T5 encoder-decoder G-stage, which the paper borrows from the HuggingFace library and does not reimplement. A weekend reimplementation of FAMAE+GAOQ is plausible for a competent ML graduate student; the full ReSID pipeline (including G-stage) requires the authors' released code.

### Lens 7 — Social Impact Assessor

The ethics subagent found no Code-of-Ethics concerns. The paper's focus on recommendation systems and tokenization efficiency has no obvious dual-use risks. The environmental impact is modest — FAMAE training is described as comparable to SASRec, and the Q-stage is dramatically faster than prior SID methods. No representational harm concerns were identified; the structured feature fields (store ID, category) are Amazon-specific and do not encode protected attributes.

### Integrated Verdict

ReSID's primary contribution — LLM-free, recommendation-native SID tokenization via FAMAE+GAOQ with an information-theoretic design framework — is genuine, clearly differentiated from all five prior SID baselines, and supported by the largest empirical evaluation in the SID literature (10 datasets, controlled ablations, efficiency analysis). Proposition 1 is mathematically sound. The "first LLM-free recommendation-native SID" and "first information-theoretic SID analysis" claims are verified. However, three material weaknesses constrain the verdict: (1) zero statistical significance testing across 52+ hypothesis cells with no seed variance reporting makes the "consistent outperforms" claim impossible to evaluate for statistical reliability; (2) asymmetric hyperparameter tuning (≥32 configs for ReSID vs. 0 for baselines) means the 16% gap over LETTER cannot be attributed to algorithmic superiority alone; (3) the task-aware metric validation uses N=2 datasets, making any correlation coefficient undefined by construction and the "reliably predicts" claim in §5.3 affirmatively misleading. The R3 DPI panel found a moderate completeness defect (hidden conditional independence assumption) that does not threaten the empirical core. The author rebuttal credibly argues that the 16% gap is 4–6× larger than the ±0.15–0.25 tuning sensitivity and that single-seed reporting is standard in this subcommunity — this magnitude argument is plausible, and the concern might be resolved by multi-seed re-running with significance tests rather than invalidating the paper's claims outright. The paper's contributions are real and the weaknesses are correctable in revision; the overall assessment is Weak Accept (ICML 3/6) with Major Revision.

### Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **(§5.1.1, §5.1.3 — Statistical significance):** Add multi-seed experiments (≥3 seeds) for the key ReSID vs. LETTER comparison and report mean ± std. Conduct paired t-tests or Wilcoxon signed-rank tests across datasets. Soften "consistently outperforms" to "on average outperforms" or "outperforms in macro-averaged results." Note: if the 10 Amazon-2023 datasets are temporal subsets of the same catalog rather than independent domain samples, the statistical tests should account for non-independence (e.g., by treating dataset as a random effect or using a within-subjects design); consult a statistician if the independence assumption is unclear.

2. **(§5.1.1, §5.1.3 — Asymmetric tuning):** Acknowledge explicitly that ReSID received dataset-specific branching-factor tuning while baselines did not, and that this asymmetry is an unquantified confound for the comparative claims. The minimum acceptable fix is a caveat; the stronger fix is matched re-tuning of at least LETTER and TIGER on Amazon-2023 data.

3. **(§5.3, Figure 3 — Metric validation):** Extend the task-aware metric correlation study to N≥5 datasets before claiming these metrics "reliably predict downstream performance." With N=2, the correlation coefficient is undefined by construction; the current claim is statistically unsupportable.

4. **(§3.1, Proposition 1 — DPI hidden assumption):** State explicitly that the DPI argument requires $H \perp\!\!\!\perp F_T \mid \mathbf{e}_T$ as a modeling assumption, alongside the existing $Y \perp\!\!\!\perp X \mid (F_T, H)$. Provide a brief justification (e.g., that field embeddings are pre-trained and fixed, making $H$ conditionally independent of $F_T$ given $\mathbf{e}_T$). Make the sufficiency bridge from $F_T$-sufficiency to $Y$-sufficiency explicit.

5. **(§4.3, Algorithm 1 — GAOQ rigor):** Either prove that Algorithm 1 optimizes Equation 4 or soften the claim from "GAOQ minimizes all three terms" to "GAOQ is designed to reduce each term via [specific mechanism]." The current claim implies an optimization guarantee that does not exist.

6. **(§5.2, Table 2 — Ablation scope):** Extend the ablation study to all 10 datasets or explain why the 3-dataset subset is representative. The current 3/10 scope makes the ablation conclusions unreliable for the paper's full 10-dataset claim.

7. **(Appendix, Table appx_sensitivity_branching — Sensitivity disclosure):** Disclose the ±0.15–0.25 R@10 hyperparameter sensitivity range in the main text's results narrative, not only in an appendix table. Practitioners implementing ReSID need to know this range to set appropriate expectations.

8. **(§5.4, Abstract — Efficiency claim scope):** Qualify the 122× speedup claim as "quantization-stage speedup" in both the abstract and §5.4, since the E-stage cost is asserted as comparable to SASRec but not measured end-to-end.

9. **(§1, §5 — Cold-start generalization):** Add cold-start evaluation: ReSID's FAMAE requires structured item features at training time, and new items without feature histories (or with unseen category/store values) cannot be reliably encoded. The paper should either (a) include a cold-start evaluation setting where items appear in the test set but not the training set, or (b) explicitly scope the claims to items seen during training and acknowledge this as a limitation of the feature-based approach.

### Questions for Authors

1. Can you provide multi-seed results (≥3 seeds) for at least the ReSID vs. LETTER comparison on Amazon-2023, with variance reported? The magnitude of the gains (16%) suggests they would survive statistical testing, which would put this concern to rest.

2. What is the theoretical or empirical basis for the conditional independence assumption $H \perp\!\!\!\perp F_T \mid \mathbf{e}_T$? In collaborative filtering, $H$ and $F_T$ share mutual information through user-item interactions — a brief justification (e.g., that $\mathbf{e}_T$ is a pre-trained fixed embedding that absorbs the user-item interaction signal) would substantially strengthen the DPI argument.

3. Would the authors consider re-tuning LETTER on Amazon-2023 data under matched compute budgets? If the 16% gap survives equal tuning, the algorithmic claim becomes unambiguous; if it narrows substantially, that is also informative and should be reported.

### Adversarial Audit Trail (R4)

The adversarial subagent advanced four strong counter-arguments: (1) the 93% FWER invalidates the "consistent outperforms" claim, not merely under-reports it; (2) the N=2 metric validation makes the "reliably predicts" claim affirmatively false by construction; (3) the abstract's "consistently outperforms" is demonstrably contradicted by the paper's own appendix data showing baseline-dataset pairs where ReSID loses or ties; (4) the DPI hidden assumption is load-bearing and false in collaborative filtering. The author rebuttal credibly addressed (1) by noting the 16% gap is 4–6× larger than the ±0.15–0.25 sensitivity range, and this magnitude argument is plausible — the concern might be resolved by multi-seed re-running rather than invalidating the claims outright. The rebuttal strongly addressed (4) by citing the standard feature-based recsys assumption $Y \perp\!\!\!\perp X \mid (F_T, H)$ and chaining it through the DPI to $Y$-sufficiency of $\mathbf{e}_T$. The rebuttal's defense of (2) (monotonic trend across checkpoints within datasets) is creative but does not resolve the architectural invalidity of N=2 cross-dataset validation. **Residual risk: the FWER concern is real and cannot be fully resolved without re-running experiments with significance tests; the asymmetric tuning concern cannot be resolved without matched re-tuning. Both should be flagged prominently in the review.**
