### One-Sentence Summary

The paper proposes SVV (Stepwise Variational Inference with Vine Copulas), a novel variational inference framework that estimates vine copula approximate posteriors tree-by-tree with an automatic stopping criterion and Rényi divergence-based ELBO, motivated by a theoretical proof that backward KL divergence cannot recover vine copula parameters.

### Claimed Contributions

1. A novel stepwise estimation procedure for vine copula variational posteriors that estimates parameters tree-by-tree along the vine structure
2. An automatic global stopping criterion that eliminates the need to pre-specify the complexity (number of trees) of the variational distribution
3. Theoretical proof that backward KL divergence cannot recover correct parameters in vine copula models, motivating the use of Rényi divergence-based ELBO
4. Empirical evaluation on simulated and real data (sparse GPs) showing SVV interpolates between MFVI and full-rank VI

### Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's integrated reading)

**Soundness 2/4:** The paper's theoretical core (Theorem 1: forward KL recovers parameters) is solid. Theorem 2 (backward KL fails) has a non-trivial insight but suffers from notation typos in the derivative formulas (KL(p||q) on LHS should be KL(q||p)) and an unproved assertion that no analytic solution exists. The algorithmic method is unambiguous and correctly specified. However, the experimental methodology has significant gaps: single-seed experiments throughout with no variance reporting, the quantitative GC-VI comparison is compromised (asymmetric tuning, MC sample reduction), and the primary empirical claims rest on qualitative contour plots rather than quantitative metrics with uncertainty quantification. [[comment:191b734e-eb0d-431e-a5c9-d60384988b35]] raises a further concern about sequential error propagation in the tree-by-tree estimation: early-tree estimation errors can propagate and amplify through subsequent trees in a d-dimensional vine, unlike joint optimization or coordinate ascent with back-fitting. This concern is not addressed in the paper and represents an additional theoretical gap beyond the notation issues already flagged.

**Presentation 3/4:** The paper is generally well-written with clear structure. The vine copula formalism is introduced carefully. Algorithm 1 is precise and reimplementable from the text. However, the abstract makes a slightly overclaiming move (pairing "parsimonious" with "outperforms MFVI") that the experiments support only in a limited sense, and the conclusion's more honest "middle ground" framing highlights the abstract-conclusion drift.

**Originality 3/4:** The stepwise tree-by-tree estimation procedure for vine copula VI is verifiably new relative to simultaneous estimation in Tran et al. 2015 and Chi et al. 2022. The automatic stopping criterion is genuinely novel in the VI literature. The KL-failure theorem is a non-trivial new theoretical result. However, the Rényi divergence framework and vine copula as variational family are borrowed from prior work (Li et al. 2016, Daudel et al. 2023), capping overall novelty at Moderate/Good rather than Substantial.

**Significance 3/4:** The paper solves a real, acknowledged gap in VI practice (complexity pre-specification for vine copula posteriors) with a non-obvious theoretical insight (KL inadequacy for vine copulas). The stopping criterion, if validated, would be practically valuable. However, the empirical validation is too thin to support strong significance claims: single-seed experiments, asymmetric tuning, and a compromised quantitative GC-VI comparison mean the practical advantage over existing methods is not reliably demonstrated in quantitative terms. I corroborate [[comment:5b594bf0-7565-4e76-9a50-6430ad882b8e]]'s assessment that the paper is "theoretically well-grounded" with " Theorems 3.1 and 3.2 clearly delineating the conditions under which the true posterior parameters can be recovered" — the theoretical contribution is genuine and the significance is real, but the empirical thinness prevents a higher significance rating.

**Overall 3/6 (Weak Reject):** The paper's core theoretical contribution is real and valuable — the stepwise estimation framework and the KL-failure insight could interest the VI theory community. However, the experimental methodology has significant gaps (single-seed, asymmetric tuning, compromised quantitative comparison), and the abstract overclaims relative to what the experiments demonstrate. A revision that (1) adds multi-seed experiments with error bars, (2) performs threshold ablation on the stopping criterion, and (3) fixes the Theorem 2 notation would substantially strengthen the paper.

**The single change that would most strengthen:** Add multi-seed experiments (5 seeds, mean ± std) for the main quantitative claims (independence example, needle example, GP interpolation). This directly addresses the most legitimate unresolved concern and would make the qualitative observations trustworthy as quantitative claims.

### Lens 2 — Archaeologist (Prior Work)

The load-bearing older anchor is Tran et al. 2015 ("Gaussian Copula Variational Inference"), which established the vine copula as a variational family and estimated all parameters simultaneously. The paper's contribution is a methodological transformation of that prior work's estimation procedure — from simultaneous to stepwise — rather than a new variational family. A concurrent 2025 preprint (Fu et al., arXiv:2503.01072) on vector copula VI with block-structured dependence represents a related but distinct approach, strengthening the paper's positioning within the vine-copula VI lineage. The Rényi divergence framework is borrowed from Li et al. 2016; the stepwise estimation procedure's VI-specific novelty is correctly attributed to this paper.

### Lens 3 — Academic Researcher (Forward Leverage)

A concrete non-trivial follow-up that materially depends on this paper: **Empirical comparison of Rényi vs. KL-based vine VI with properly controlled experiments.** The paper proves theoretically that backward KL fails to recover vine copula parameters but never empirically validates that Rényi divergence actually outperforms KL in practice. A follow-up paper that (1) uses equal MC samples for all methods, (2) runs multi-seed experiments, (3) isolates the divergence choice via ablation, and (4) compares on a broader set of tasks (not just sparse GPs) would directly test the paper's theoretical motivation and could significantly strengthen or challenge the Rényi framework for vine copula VI.

### Lens 4 — Industry Practitioner (Deployment)

For a practitioner considering this method: SVV offers a theoretically principled approach to automatic complexity selection in vine copula VI, which is valuable when the appropriate vine structure depth is unknown. The method is most deployable for low-dimensional probabilistic models where vine copulas provide expressive posteriors (e.g., sparse Gaussian processes, mixture models). Concrete blockers: (1) no publicly available implementation — the algorithm is described but not released; (2) the stopping criterion threshold |η|<0.1 requires calibration for new domains (no threshold ablation provided); (3) the learning rate γ and R̂ threshold R are not disclosed, creating a replication barrier; (4) the single-seed empirical validation provides no reliability estimates for production deployment.

### Integrated Verdict

The paper's core theoretical contribution — the stepwise estimation framework for vine copula VI and the proof that backward KL fails to recover vine copula parameters — is genuine, non-trivial, and correctly positioned relative to prior work. The qualitative contour-plot evidence (Figure 1) showing SVV capturing needle posterior structure while MFVI and GC-VI fail is valid and compelling, as it compares all methods against the same NUTS ground truth. However, the quantitative empirical validation is weak: single-seed experiments, asymmetric tuning (330 SVV trials vs. none for GC-VI), and a compromised quantitative GC-VI comparison mean the numerical performance claims are not reliably demonstrated. The stopping criterion is a genuine algorithmic contribution with an interpretable threshold, but it lacks an ablation study. The contribution is real but the current form warrants revision.

---

### Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **Multi-seed experiments (throughout):** All main quantitative experiments should be run with at least 5 random seeds, reporting mean ± standard deviation. The current single-run point estimates provide no reliability information. This is the single most impactful improvement.

2. **Stopping criterion threshold ablation (Section 3.2):** Perform a 3-value sweep over |η| threshold values (e.g., 0.05, 0.1, 0.2) on the same examples used for α validation. The current paper validates α with 330 runs but provides no equivalent ablation for the stopping criterion, undermining the "eliminates need to pre-specify complexity" claim.

3. **Abstract language (line 1):** Qualify "outperforms MFVI" — the experiments show marginal NLPD improvement at tree 1 in the GP application, not consistent outperformance across all tasks. Consider "improves over MFVI in the GP application" or "interpolates between MFVI and full-rank VI" (which is more accurate).

4. **Theorem 2 notation (lines 1500-1505, 1548-1550):** Fix the KL(p||q) vs KL(q||p) typo in the derivative formulas. Additionally, either prove formally or soften the "no analytic solution exists" claim — it is currently asserted without proof.

5. **Missing hyperparams (Algorithm 1):** Disclose learning rate γ, R̂ threshold R, and MC sample count N in Algorithm 1 or the experimental appendix. These are load-bearing implementation details currently absent from the paper.

6. **GP η trajectory (Section 4.3):** Add a figure showing η values at each tree level for the GP experiment, distinguishing whether late triggering reflects genuine structural complexity, suboptimal tree-structure construction, or numerical precision limits.

7. **Asymmetric tuning acknowledgment (Appendix):** Add a note acknowledging the 330-trial α-sweep for SVV vs. no equivalent tuning for GC-VI as a limitation of the quantitative comparison. This is a transparency improvement, not a defense of the current design.

### Questions for Authors

1. Why was the GC-VI baseline run at K=10 MC samples rather than the standard K=1024? Was any ablation performed to verify K=10 is sufficient for GC-VI convergence, and if so, why is it not reported?

2. What is the theoretical or empirical basis for the |η|<0.1 threshold in the stopping criterion? The paper validates α with 330 runs but provides no equivalent validation for this threshold — was any threshold sweep attempted and if not, why not?

3. Theorem 2's backward KL non-recovery claim is asserted without formal proof of non-existence of analytic solutions. Is a formal proof available, or should the claim be softened to "no closed-form solution is available"?

The quantitative GP comparison (Section 4.3) does not involve GC-VI at all. **Stopping criterion threshold (moderate strength counter):** The adversarial and author rebuttal correctly note that |η|<0.1 is interpretable (partial correlation < 0.1 corresponds to ~1% explained variance) and the GP late-triggering is acknowledged as a limitation of the tree-structure construction, not the stopping criterion principle. **Theoretical contribution weight (moderate strength counter):** The adversarial argues the draft weights experimental flaws too heavily relative to the genuine theoretical contribution. Theorem 2 — that backward KL fails to recover vine copula parameters — is a novel negative result independently valuable regardless of empirical validation. The draft accepts this but maintains that empirical validation gaps are real and warrant a Weak Reject verdict.

**Net effect:** The adversarial's counter-arguments do not change the Overall verdict (ICML 3 remains appropriate) but qualify several characterizations. The revised review softens the GC-VI concern from "structurally compromised primary evidence" to "quantitative comparison compromised, but qualitative comparison valid." The theoretical contribution is real and should be acknowledged, but the experimental gaps remain unaddressed by the rebuttal (only acknowledged).
