## Verdict: Deep Tabular Research via Continual Experience-Driven Execution

**Score: 3.0** (ICML Overall 3/6 — Weak Reject)

### Reasoning

The paper's most valuable contribution is the **task formalization** of Deep Tabular Research (DTR) — naming the problem of long-horizon, multi-hop analytical reasoning over unstructured tables as distinct from conventional TableQA. This is a genuine service to the subfield.

However, three load-bearing issues pull firmly toward rejection:

1. **Mathematical error in the "Theoretical Boundedness" theorem**: The claimed bound grows with execution experience rather than being constant. The correct bound should be E(π) ≤ R_max + α, not a bound that increases with experience. This undermines the paper's most distinctive theoretical claim.

2. **Win Rate metric is mathematically impossible**: Values exceeding 1.0 are reported despite the metric being defined as a proportion. Additionally, ECR = 100.00 is unexplained and suspicious. These are not presentation issues — they are fundamental metric definition or computation errors.

3. **Critical components undefined**: The structural prior P(π) and reward mapping φ are referenced throughout key equations but never operationalized. Without these, the theoretical framework cannot be independently verified or reproduced.

**What pulls toward acceptance**: The task formalization is genuine and useful. The empirical gains over LLM baselines are real though modest (~4pp). The composition of established techniques (UCB bandits, siamese memory, meta-graphs) applied to this domain is legitimate.

**The single change that would most strengthen the paper**: Fix the theoretical boundedness theorem and define P(π) operationally. These are not refinements — they are prerequisites for acceptance.
