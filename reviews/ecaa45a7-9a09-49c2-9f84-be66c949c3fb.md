### One-Sentence Summary
The paper develops a Feynman-diagram-like formal power series expansion for analyzing gradient flow dynamics in CP tensor decomposition, classifying learning regimes (free/NTK/mean-field/under/over-parameterized) as faces of a Pareto-optimal scaling polygon and deriving explicit closed-form solutions for multiple regimes—validating visually against identity-tensor targets at small p values.

### Claimed Contributions
1. A power series expansion of loss evolution in time, with coefficients encoded by Feynman-like diagrams
2. Pareto-optimal analysis of large-size scaling limits yielding a geometric "Pareto polygon" classifying learning regimes  
3. Association of different learning regimes (NTK, mean-field, lazy, rich) with different faces of the Pareto polygon
4. A general method for summing formal power series by reducing to a first-order PDE solvable by method of characteristics
5. Explicit closed-form solutions for free evolution (zero target) in under- and over-parameterized settings
6. Explicit solution for the NTK regime in asymmetric (ASYM) CP models, with proof that NTK is absent in symmetric (SYM) even-v models
7. Complete solution for the symmetric matrix model valid for any double limit of p and H
8. Analysis of unlearning regimes in 4th-order identity tensor decomposition

### Disagreement-Resolution Narratives

AST downgraded from Critical after accepting SS11 disclosure genuine and core theorems convergence-independent. The adversarial subagent countered that Borel summation without convergence proof is standard in formal asymptotics literature (citing the Feynman diagram parallel). The committee's response: this is correct that the methodology is standard in physics but the paper presents these as explicit closed-form solutions rather than as formal asymptotic predictions, and the experimental validation lacks error bars to compensate. Remedy: abstract caveat solutions are asymptotic predictions pending convergence proof.

### Integrated Verdict
This is a solid theoretical ML paper with genuine methodological novelty (diagram/Pareto framework for gradient flow analysis), mathematically rigorous core contributions (Theorems 3.1 and 4.1), and rare explicit closed-form solutions for nonlinear learning dynamics across multiple regimes. The highest-confidence committee concern is the abstract-scope-overclaim: the abstract claims a "general mathematical framework for large learning problems" but all experiments validate only CP decomposition of identity tensors at p<=512, and the main theorems carry nu restrictions (even nu for Theorem 4.1, nu=2 for Proposition 8.1) not disclosed in the abstract. This is scope inflation on two simultaneous layers. The formal series convergence gap is real and load-bearing but is disclosed in Section 11 and is standard practice in formal asymptotics literature. The Table 2 error (C-D ASYM labeled Lazy instead of Rich) is a corrigendum-level issue. These concerns are correctable through revision; none rises to rejection level. The paper is a Weak Accept whose camera-ready should require: (1) abstract revision matching demonstrated scope, (2) explicit convergence caveat in abstract and Section 7, (3) formal correction of Table 2.
