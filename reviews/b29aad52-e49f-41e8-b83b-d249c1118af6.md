## One-Sentence Summary

RetroReasoner proposes a chemist-inspired stepwise bond-disconnection reasoning process for retrosynthesis prediction, trained via SyntheticRetro-generated SFT data and GRPO RL with round-trip accuracy reward, demonstrating improved accuracy and route diversity over molecular LLMs — with a correctable 10× numerical inflation in the hard-instance evaluation table, a reporting gap in statistical significance testing, and a disclosed round-trip reward circularity that is mitigated by separate model sizes but requires validation.

---

## Claimed Contributions

1. A stepwise reasoning process following chemists' bond-disconnection strategy, and SyntheticRetro — a framework that converts this process into structured reasoning text data for SFT.
2. RetroReasoner: trained with SyntheticRetro SFT data + GRPO RL with round-trip accuracy reward, enabling broader and more feasible reactant proposals.
3. Empirical validation demonstrating effectiveness on in-distribution, rare-reaction-template, and rare-atom/token evaluation subsets.

### Disagreement-Resolution Narrative

**Panel 1 (hard-instance-delta-inflation):** All 5 panelists converged on the factual finding: 10× delta inflation is verified arithmetic. The debate centered on severity and causal mechanism. The hyperparameter-budget-auditor's causal chain (asymmetric tuning → inflation concentrated on hardest conditions) was compelling but not definitively proven. The statistician's power analysis (N=100 insufficient for true +0.02 effect) was universally accepted as compounding the problem. Resolution: mandatory corrections required; methodology contribution is genuine and separable.

**Panel 2 (statistical-significance-absence):** 5/5 agreed this is a structural design flaw, not a reporting gap. The 10× inflation is a consequence of absent statistical discipline (no internal check to catch implausible deltas). Dispute on severity label was not resolved ("Fundamentally Compromised" vs. "Significant gaps" vs. "Concerning anomalies") but all agreed the hard-instance claims are doubly compromised. Resolution: multi-seed reruns + statistical testing required.

**Panel 3 (roundtrip-reward-circularity):** 5/5 agreed on the structural circularity. Dispute: is the Feasible Ratio regression (0.786 vs 0.812) evidence of mechanism failure or "broader exploration"? The paper offers no analysis to resolve this. Resolution: an independent forward model for evaluation is the only clean fix; Exact@1 claims survive the circularity critique.

**Panel 4 (hyperparameter-asymmetry-prediction-only):** 4/5 agreed the ablation design is methodologically sound. 5/5 agreed RL-specific hyperparameters are inapplicable to Prediction-Only's SFT-only training. Dispute on whether this is "Demonstrably Unfair" or "Asymmetric tuning suspected" — resolved as the latter. Resolution: sensitivity analysis required; direction of advantage is demonstrated; magnitude is "Asserted" not "Supported."

**Panel 5 (template-diversity-conflation):** 5/5 agreed the conflation is real. Dispute on severity: 4 panelists classified as significant deficiency; significance subagent classified as correctable precision issue. Resolution: disclosure fix (single sentence in abstract) is sufficient; the narrow form (route diversity) is demonstrated; the broad form (feasible space coverage) is over-interpreted.

## Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's Integrated Reading)

### Soundness: 2/4

The paper has genuine methodological strengths: a well-motivated reasoning taxonomy (R1-R4), a properly isolated ablation control (Prediction-Only), a chemically sensible RL reward design (round-trip accuracy), and comprehensive metric coverage. However, the empirical foundation has three compounding concerns: (1) a verified 10× numerical inflation in two cells of the hard-instance evaluation table (correctable in camera-ready); (2) absence of statistical significance testing or variance reporting across all 90+ implicit comparisons; and (3) an asymmetric hyperparameter tuning setup for the Prediction-Only baseline. The corrected hard-instance deltas (+0.02, +0.01) are likely underpowered at N=100. The round-trip reward has a disclosed structural circularity (f_φ as both RL signal and evaluator) that is partially mitigated by separate model sizes (0.6B reward / 8B eval) but requires further validation.

The R4 adversarial pass correctly notes that the in-distribution results (main_ID.tex) are arithmetic-clean and are the paper's primary empirical contribution. The 10× inflation is confined to exactly 2 of 22 delta cells in a single subtable. The statistical absence is the field norm, not a unique flaw. The round-trip circularity is disclosed and partially mitigated. These considerations soften but do not eliminate the concerns.

### Presentation: 2/4

The paper is generally well-organized with clear section structure, good figures, and mathematically precise metric definitions. However, the 10× delta inflation in Table `main_hard.tex` is a reader-facing factual error — the table caption explicitly directs readers to use these deltas as the performance comparison. The abstract's "broader feasible set" claim is imprecise but is directly supported by Feasible Ratio (the metric measuring proportion of feasible candidates); Template Diversity provides a complementary characterization of route diversity. Three minor typos exist. The paper discloses the hyperparameter sharing for Prediction-Only.

### Originality: 3/4

The SyntheticRetro data generation pipeline (GPT-oss-20B generating n=15 diverse linking texts per reaction with 4-step R1-R4 taxonomy) is genuinely novel in its specific form. The R1-R4 structured bond-disconnection reasoning taxonomy is distinct from prior molecular LLMs that do generic product analysis. The round-trip accuracy as RL reward is anticipated by Kong et al. 2025 and RetroDFM-R (July 2025), but the retrosynthesis-specific application with strategic disconnection reasoning is a distinct contribution.

### Significance: 3/4

Retrosynthesis prediction is an important problem in organic synthesis. The paper demonstrates that structured, chemistry-specific strategic reasoning improves both accuracy and route diversity of reactant predictions. The practical impact is constrained by no released artifacts, dependence on GPT-oss-20B for linking text generation, and moderate-sized target community. The forward-leverage follow-up (ForwardRetro for forward reaction prediction) is concrete and non-trivial.

### Overall: 3/6 (Weak Accept — borderline, salvageable with corrections)

The methodology is a genuine contribution. The empirical claims for the primary contribution (in-distribution results, ablation studies) are sound. The hard-instance robustness claims are compromised by numerical inflation and statistical absence but are correctable. The round-trip reward evaluation has a disclosed circularity that is partially mitigated. The paper is salvageable with mandatory corrections to the hard-instance table, multi-seed statistical validation, and round-trip reward revalidation — but cannot be accepted in its current form without the 10× delta correction.

**The single change that would most strengthen the paper:** Correct the 10× inflated deltas in Table `main_hard.tex`, run multi-seed evaluation (≥3 seeds) with variance reporting and statistical significance tests, and independently retune Prediction-Only on the hard-instance distribution.

## Integrated Verdict

RetroReasoner makes a genuine methodological contribution: a structured bond-disconnection reasoning taxonomy (R1-R4) for retrosynthesis, implemented via SyntheticRetro-generated SFT data and GRPO RL with round-trip accuracy reward. The core algorithmic contribution is sound and novel in its specific form. The in-distribution empirical results (main_ID.tex) are arithmetic-clean and show consistent, meaningful gains across multiple metrics. The hard-instance evaluation table has a verified 10× numerical inflation in two cells (correctable in camera-ready) and lacks statistical significance testing, making the hard-instance Exact@1 advantage (+0.02) statistically unsubstantiated at N=100. The round-trip reward evaluation has a disclosed structural circularity that is partially mitigated by separate model sizes (0.6B reward / 8B eval) but requires validation with an independent forward model. The paper is salvageable with mandatory corrections — the core contribution is real and the empirical presentation issues are correctable.

## Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **Table `main_hard.tex` Rare Template Exact@1**: Correct the parenthetical delta values. SFT: (+0.20) → (+0.02). RL: (+0.10) → (+0.01). This is a camera-ready fix, not a methodological revision.

2. **Statistical significance testing**: Report variance across ≥3 random seeds for all main results. Add bootstrap confidence intervals for primary deltas. The corrected Exact@1 deltas (+0.02, +0.01) should be explicitly disclosed as potentially underpowered at N=100.

3. **Round-trip reward validation**: Report the 0.6B forward synthesis model's held-out accuracy. Confirm train-test disjointness between f_φ training data and evaluation data. Consider using an independent (non-RL-trained) forward model for evaluation to break the circularity.

4. **Abstract framing**: Revise to "generates a broader range of feasible reactant proposals with greater route diversity" to eliminate ambiguity. The current phrasing is imprecise but is supported by Feasible Ratio as the direct metric.

5. **§7 Limitations**: Expand to explicitly discuss: (a) multi-label instances excluded from evaluation (disclosed in §6.3 but not in Limitations); (b) single-seed evaluation limits generalizability; (c) round-trip reward's dependence on forward model accuracy.

6. **Prediction-Only hyperparameter sensitivity**: Run Prediction-Only across 3–5 learning rate configurations on the hard-instance distribution and report the range of Exact@1 values. The current single-setting comparison cannot establish robustness to hyperparameter choice.

7. **SMILES canonicalization**: Use a canonical SMILES library (e.g., RDKit) before exact string comparison in the round-trip reward to eliminate systematic false negatives from non-canonical representations.

## Questions for Authors

1. Can you provide the raw multi-seed data (≥3 seeds) for the hard-instance evaluation? The reported +0.02 delta may not be statistically distinguishable from zero at N=100, and without seed-level data this cannot be verified.

2. What is the held-out accuracy of the 0.6B forward synthesis model (f_φ) used for training-time reward computation? Is its training data disjoint from the ORDerly evaluation split?

3. The Feasible Ratio is lower with round-trip reward (0.786) than without (0.812) in the ablation table. The paper attributes this to "broader exploration" but provides no analysis. Can you explain this regression and provide an analysis of whether this reflects reward signal corruption or genuine strategic diversification?

## Adversarial Audit Trail (R4)

The R4 adversarial pass raised four counter-arguments:

1. **10× inflation is isolated**: Confirmed — the inflation is confined to 2 cells in `main_hard.tex` Rare Template Exact@1. The in-distribution table (`main_ID.tex`) is arithmetic-clean. The paper's primary contribution (methodological pipeline + in-distribution results) is not invalidated. **Assessment**: Valid — the correction is a camera-ready fix, not a methodological revision. The directionality of all findings is preserved.

2. **Statistical absence is field norm**: Partially valid — the absence is a reporting gap, not a structural flaw, and the field norm is the same practice. However, the 10× inflation demonstrates that this paper specifically lacked the internal check (variance/CI) that would have caught the error. **Assessment**: Valid — the severity framing ("Fundamentally Compromised") is too strong; "Reporting gaps" is more accurate. Multi-seed reruns are a revision requirement, not a rejection trigger.

3. **Round-trip circularity is disclosed and mitigated**: Partially valid — the 8B/0.6B model separation and train-test disjointness are genuine mitigating factors. The Exact@1 metric is fully independent of f_φ. **Assessment**: Valid — the circularity concern applies only to round-trip-based metrics, not to the primary accuracy claims. An independent forward model for evaluation remains the cleanest fix.

4. **Template Diversity conflation is supported by Feasible Ratio**: Valid — Feasible Ratio (0.625 vs. 0.621 RL on Rare Template; 0.557 vs. 0.478 RL on Rare Atom/Token) directly supports "broader feasible set." Template Diversity contextualizes this as route diversity. **Assessment**: Valid — the abstract claim is defensible; the presentation improvement is a refinement, not a correction.

**Net R4 assessment**: The adversarial counter-argument is strong on points 1 and 4, moderate on points 2 and 3. The paper's core contribution survives the adversarial pass. The appropriate verdict is **Weak Accept with mandatory corrections** — not a rejection, but not an unconditional accept.

**Residual risk**: If the 0.6B forward model's training data overlaps with the ORDerly test set, the round-trip reward signal is compromised and the RL-trained model's advantages may be partially artifactual. This risk is disclosed but not quantified. The multi-seed reruns will reveal whether the hard-instance Exact@1 +0.02 delta is reproducible or noise.
