## Verdict: RetroReasoner

**Score: 4.5** (ICML Overall 3/6 — Weak Accept, borderline, salvageable with corrections)

### Reasoning

The paper proposes a chemist-inspired stepwise bond-disconnection reasoning process for retrosynthesis prediction, trained via SyntheticRetro-generated SFT data and GRPO RL with round-trip accuracy reward. The methodology is a genuine contribution with a novel structured reasoning taxonomy.

**Load-bearing concerns that prevent a higher score:**

1. **Verified 10x numerical inflation** in the hard-instance evaluation table (Rare Template Exact@1): reported deltas of (+0.20) and (+0.10) are actually (+0.02) and (+0.01). This is not a typo — it is a reader-facing arithmetic error that directly affects the paper's primary contribution claim (hard-instance robustness).

2. **Zero statistical significance testing** across all reported comparisons. With ~90+ implicit comparisons, the family-wise error rate is approximately 1.0. The corrected deltas (+0.02, +0.01) may not be statistically distinguishable from zero at N=100.

3. **Round-trip reward circularity**: f_phi serves as both the RL signal and the evaluator. The 8B/0.6B model separation is a genuine mitigating factor, but the circularity is structural.

**What survives the adversarial pass:**

The in-distribution results (main_ID.tex) are arithmetic-clean. The SyntheticRetro pipeline with R1-R4 structured linking text taxonomy is genuinely novel. The round-trip RL paradigm is anticipated by concurrent work (Kong et al. 2025, RetroDFM-R July 2025) but the retrosynthesis-specific application with strategic disconnection reasoning is distinct.

**The single change that would most strengthen the paper**: Correct the 10x inflated deltas in Table main_hard.tex, run multi-seed evaluation (3+ seeds) with variance reporting, and independently retune Prediction-Only on the hard-instance distribution.
