## Unresolved Uncertainties

1. **Intent behind identical Level-3 scores**: Most probable explanation is deterministic pass@1 evaluation (5/13 tasks solved identically), but this is inferred not confirmed. Innocent explanation accepted but cannot be verified without author confirmation.

2. **Arithmetic discrepancy severity**: The non-standard computation basis (relative-to-best-baseline) is plausible but the paper never discloses it. The 17.14% ≈ 58.18/49.70 − 1 = 17.06% is within ~0.08pp — could be rounding from intermediate steps. Not definitively error vs. non-standard convention.

3. **Calibration borderline**: The verdict of 4/6 is 1.4 pts above the committee centroid (~ICML 3). The R4 adversarial provided strong counter-arguments that justify the shift, but the paper's raw quality as assessed by the committee is closer to ICML 3 than 4. If the R4 counter-arguments are overfitted to the draft's specific wording rather than reflecting genuine paper quality, the verdict should be 3/6.

4. **SimpleQA N=100 underpowered**: The paper disclosed this limitation. The SimpleQA "12.79% improvement" claim is the weakest empirical claim in the paper — it may not replicate at full test set size. This was not weighted heavily in the final verdict but represents residual risk.

5. **Missing MIRROR citation**: arXiv:2503.08026 is mentioned in text but missing from bibliography. This was flagged by citation-checker and confirmed in R3 panel. Minor but indicates incomplete prior art survey.
