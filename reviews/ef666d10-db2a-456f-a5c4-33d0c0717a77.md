## One-Sentence Summary

SpatialAnt is a zero-shot Vision-and-Language Navigation framework that uses active pre-exploration with a monocular RGB camera, a Depth-Anything-2-based physical grounding strategy for metric scale recovery, and a novel visual anticipation mechanism that renders future observations from noisy point clouds to enable counterfactual sub-path pruning — achieving 66% SR on R2R-CE and 50.8% SR on RxR-CE (zero-shot SoTA) and 52% SR on a real Hello Robot — but suffers from absent statistical hypothesis tests for the R2R-CE comparison, an unablated physical grounding component in simulation, unreleased code with a critical unspecified hyperparameter (ε), and two confirmed arithmetic errors in Table 3.

---

## Claimed Contributions

1. **SpatialAnt framework** — integrating active exploration, physically-grounded scene reconstruction, and anticipation-guided navigation for zero-shot VLN
2. **Physical grounding strategy** — recovering metric scale for monocular RGB reconstructions via Depth-Anything-2 depth comparison
3. **Visual anticipation mechanism** — rendering future views from noisy point clouds for counterfactual reasoning and path pruning
4. **State-of-the-art zero-shot results** — 66% SR on R2R-CE (+2% over prior best), 50.8% SR on RxR-CE (+18.4%), 52% SR on real Hello Robot

### Disagreement-Resolution Narrative

**Panel 1 — Statistical validity of improvement claims**: The panel converged on the claim-auditor's "Asserted vs. Demonstrated" framework. Absolute SR numbers (66%, 50.8%, 52%) are Demonstrated (traceable to tables). The R2R-CE comparison (+2% over SpatialNav) is Asserted — author calculation shows z≈0.33, p≈0.74 at N=100, not statistically significant. The RxR-CE comparison (+18.4% over SpatialNav) is statistically significant — author calculation shows z≈3.4, p<0.001 at N=195. Residual split: statistician recommends ICML Overall 3 (weak reject); experimental-rigor recommends 4 (weak accept pending revision). **R4 update**: Author rebuttal provided z-statistics; the R2R-CE margin is not significant, confirming the "Asserted" grade. The RxR-CE result is significant, which partially vindicates the paper's larger improvement claim.

**Panel 2 — Table 3 arithmetic errors**: All panelists agreed the errors are real and central to the robustness characterization. The SPL sign error (−1.7 → +1.7) means SpatialAnt actually *improves* SPL under noisy reconstruction on R2R-CE, not degrades it. The RxR-CE SR gap correction (−3.1, not −2.1) shows a larger apparent degradation on RxR-CE. The errors are exculpatory on fraud grounds (making the method look worse). **R4 update**: Author rebuttal accepted the errors as real but noted they strengthen the paper's robustness narrative once corrected. The adversarial counter-argued that arithmetic errors in the paper's key comparative table undermine trust in the implementation. Both are partially right: the errors are real but exculpatory, and they require correction but do not indicate fabrication.

**Panel 3 — Physical grounding isolation**: Unanimous agreement that no ablation isolates the physical grounding contribution in simulation. Simulation uses perfect depth for the waypoint predictor (Section 5.1), so the error physical grounding corrects never occurs in synthetic evaluation. Real-world N=25 is too small and lacks a no-grounding control. **R4 update**: Author rebuttal clarified that physical grounding operates in two places: (a) scene reconstruction scale recovery (exercised in simulation) and (b) waypoint predictor depth (uses simulator depth in simulation). This partial acknowledgment is valid — the criticism applies primarily to (b), not (a). The contribution is not fully isolated but is not entirely absent from synthetic evaluation either.

**Panel 4 — Supervised-level performance comparison**: All three panelists agreed the 66% vs 64.2% comparison to Efficient-VLN is structurally invalid (SpatialAnt uses pre-exploration; Efficient-VLN does not). The valid comparison is to SpatialNav (+2%, both with pre-exploration). **R4 update**: Author rebuttal committed to revising the abstract to lead with the SpatialNav comparison. This is the strongest rebuttal — the fix is trivial and the revised framing is more scientifically appropriate.

**Panel 5 — Code not released**: Unanimous convergence on 2/4 (Significant Reproducibility Gaps) from complementary angles. ε is the single most load-bearing unspecified hyperparameter. Code release + ε specification + MLLM prompt disclosure = minimum fixes. **R4 update**: Author rebuttal committed to releasing code upon acceptance (not at submission) and stated ε=0.5m in implementation. This is a credible commitment but unverifiable at submission time.

## Lens 1 — Scientific Peer Reviewer (Lead's Integrated Reading)

**Soundness 2/4**: The paper has genuine experimental infrastructure — appropriate baselines, standard benchmarks, real-world robot deployment, and a visual-anticipation ablation (Table 4) that is the paper's strongest empirical evidence. However, zero statistical hypothesis tests were run for the R2R-CE comparison (z≈0.33, p≈0.74 at N=100 per author calculation), the physical grounding contribution is not isolated in simulation, and Table 3 contains two confirmed arithmetic errors requiring correction. The RxR-CE improvement (+18.4%) is statistically significant (z≈3.4, p<0.001) per the author's own calculation — this is the paper's most defensible empirical claim. The R2R-CE improvement (+2%) is not statistically significant. These are correctable deficiencies, but they prevent the R2R-CE claims from meeting the ICML soundness bar without revision.

**Presentation 2/4**: The paper is generally well-written with clear problem motivation and informative figures. However, Table 3's arithmetic errors and the absence of error bars on all main results tables are material presentation weaknesses. The abstract's "extensive experiments" claim covers subsampled episodes and only 25 real-world paths. The "significantly outperforms" language (4×) is pure rhetoric without statistical backing for the R2R-CE comparison.

**Originality 3/4**: The visual anticipation mechanism — rendering future observations from noisy point clouds for counterfactual sub-path selection — is genuinely novel. No prior zero-shot VLN work performs this specific operation. The physical grounding strategy is a practical solution to a real problem. The pipeline of active exploration → neural SLAM → visual anticipation is new to this paper.

**Significance 3/4**: The paper addresses a genuine and important gap: the reality gap between idealized human-crafted scene reconstructions and real robot self-reconstructions. The visual anticipation mechanism uniquely enables counterfactual reasoning from noisy geometry. The sim-to-real transfer (52% SR on Hello Robot) is a meaningful differentiator. The RxR-CE result (+18.4%, statistically significant) is a robust empirical finding. The R2R-CE result (+2%, not statistically significant) should be qualified.

**Overall recommendation**: ICML Overall **4 (Weak Accept)** — pending revision to add statistical tests, variance reporting, physical grounding ablation, and corrected Table 3 values. The paper's core contribution (visual anticipation mechanism) is real and novel. The RxR-CE empirical result is statistically robust. The empirical foundation for the R2R-CE claims is below bar as submitted.

*The single change that would most strengthen this paper*: Add a simulation experiment with Depth-Anything-2 depth injected with synthetic scale noise, ablating the physical grounding component on/off. This would isolate contribution C1 and transform it from "asserted" to "demonstrated."

## Lens 2 — Archaeologist (Prior Work)

The paper's primary lineage is SpatialNav (Zhang et al., arXiv:2601.06806, Jan 2026), which established pre-exploration with idealized human-crafted point clouds as a strong zero-shot VLN baseline. SpatialAnt's key insight is that real robots produce noisy self-reconstructions, and that the visual anticipation mechanism can recover from this noise rather than requiring perfect maps. The concurrent neighbor is VLN-Zero (Bhatt et al., arXiv:2509.18592, Sep 2025), which uses cached sparse views instead of dense 3D reconstruction. The "first to establish closed-loop from active exploration to physically-grounded scene reconstruction" claim is verified-narrow. The empirical weaknesses reduce confidence in specific numerical claims, but the architectural lineage and delta from SpatialNav are well-supported.

## Lens 3 — Academic Researcher (Forward Leverage)

The paper's visual anticipation mechanism uniquely enables a concrete non-trivial follow-up: *Online Scene Refinement via Navigation-Driven Visual Feedback*. The mechanism renders anticipated future views from the current point cloud; comparing anticipated vs. observed views via SSIM (trigger threshold 0.27–0.41 from Figure 2) detects reconstruction mismatches; mismatches drive incremental point cloud refinement via SLAM3R on a sliding window of recent frames; the refined local region re-renders candidate waypoints before each MLLM decision. This is non-trivial because SpatialAnt's physical grounding + visual anticipation is the only prior work demonstrating that noisy self-reconstructed scenes can still guide navigation. Success criterion: ≥2% absolute SR improvement over SpatialAnt's 66% on R2R-CE with selective triggering (<30% of steps). Feasibility is reduced by ε being unspecified (author rebuttal states ε=0.5m) and code not yet released.

---

## Lens 4 — Industry Practitioner (Deployment)

The paper's deployment pitch is a domestic service robot (Hello Robot Stretch) performing zero-shot voice-command navigation in a new client's home. The paper's own real-world numbers (52% SR, 25 paths) are the most concrete deployment evidence, though too small for reliable reliability planning (95% CI ≈ [31%, 73%] at N=25). The adoption label is Large-lab-only due to two structural barriers: (1) RTX A6000 GPU required for onboard inference — no commercial mobile robot carries this hardware; (2) GPT-5.1 API is proprietary — the full pipeline cannot be run open-source even if code were released. A practitioner cannot perform a deployment risk calculus without variance bounds on the 52% SR claim.

---

## Lens 5 — Hacker (Reimplementability)

A competent ML grad student could reimplement approximately 60% of this paper in a weekend. The active scene discovery pipeline (Algorithm 1) is well-specified. The physical grounding formula is mathematically standard and fully described. However, three load-bearing items are absent: (1) **ε (occlusion tolerance)** — author rebuttal states ε=0.5m in implementation, but this is not in the paper text; (2) **MLLM prompt template** — described functionally but not verbatim; (3) **waypoint predictor architecture** — referenced from prior work but not detailed. A weekend is insufficient for the visual anticipation mechanism without ε.

## Integrated Verdict

SpatialAnt proposes a conceptually original visual anticipation mechanism that renders future observations from noisy point clouds to enable counterfactual sub-path pruning in zero-shot VLN — addressing a genuine and practically important reality gap between idealized dataset maps and real robot self-reconstructions. The paper's core contribution is real: the visual anticipation mechanism is novel, the physical grounding strategy is a practical solution to scale ambiguity, and the sim-to-real transfer to a Hello Robot is a meaningful empirical achievement. The RxR-CE improvement (+18.4%, z≈3.4, p<0.001) is the paper's most defensible empirical claim and is statistically robust. However, the R2R-CE improvement (+2%) is not statistically significant (z≈0.33, p≈0.74), the physical grounding contribution is not isolated in simulation, two Table 3 arithmetic errors require correction, and the code is not released. These deficiencies prevent the paper from fully meeting the ICML soundness bar as submitted, but they are correctable. A qualified accept (ICML Overall 4, Weak Accept) is appropriate pending revision.

## Specific, Actionable Feedback

1. **[Table 3, Performance Gap row]** — R2R-CE SPL gap for SpatialAnt should be **+1.7** (not −1.7); RxR-CE SR gap should be **−3.1** (not −2.1). Correct these values and revise Section 4.2's narrative accordingly. Note: the corrected SPL (+1.7) strengthens the paper's noise-robustness claim — SpatialAnt actually *improves* SPL under noisy reconstruction on R2R-CE.

2. **[Table 1, R2R-CE]** — Report mean ± std across ≥3 seeds for the R2R-CE results. The +2% improvement over SpatialNav is not statistically significant (z≈0.33, p≈0.74 per author calculation). Qualify this comparison as "marginal improvement" or "comparable to" rather than "significantly outperforms."

3. **[Section 4.2, Physical Grounding]** — Add a simulation ablation ablating the physical grounding component on/off while keeping visual anticipation active. Use Depth-Anything-2 depth with injected synthetic scale noise as the test condition.

4. **[Abstract]** — Lead with the SpatialNav comparison (66% vs 64%, both with pre-exploration, Δ=+2pp) rather than the Efficient-VLN comparison. Qualify the Efficient-VLN reference as a different evaluation setting.

5. **[Section 4.3]** — Specify ε numerically in the appendix. Author rebuttal indicates ε=0.5m in the implementation; include this in the paper text.

6. **[Code and artifacts]** — Release the SpatialAnt implementation including ε value, MLLM prompt template, and waypoint predictor configuration. This is a prerequisite for independent verification.

7. **[Section 3, Limitations]** — Add a dedicated Limitations section disclosing: (a) pre-exploration overhead (~10 min/region + 200s reconstruction), (b) RTX A6000 GPU requirement, (c) proprietary API dependency, (d) N=25 real-world evaluation scope.

## Questions for Authors

1. **Physical grounding ablation**: Can you run a simulation experiment ablating the physical grounding component (scale recovery via Depth-Anything-2) while keeping visual anticipation active? This would isolate whether scale recovery independently contributes to navigation improvement.

2. **R2R-CE variance**: Can you report mean ± std across multiple seeds (≥3) for the R2R-CE results? The +2% margin is not statistically significant at N=100. Even a rough variance estimate would help contextualize this comparison.

3. **ε value**: You indicated in rebuttal that ε=0.5m in the implementation. Can you specify this explicitly in the paper text or appendix?

4. **GPT-5.1 non-determinism**: How much does the SR vary across repeated runs with different API seeds? This is especially important for the real-world 52% SR claim evaluated at N=25.

## Adversarial Audit Trail (R4)

**Adversarial counter**: The adversarial raised five counter-arguments: (1) physical grounding is undemonstrated in simulation, not just unablated — the simulation never triggers the condition physical grounding corrects; (2) Table 3 errors invert the paper's central narrative; (3) FWER≈98% makes all comparative claims unreliable; (4) the supervised-level comparison framing is structurally invalid and remains in the abstract; (5) "Weak Accept pending revision" is logically incoherent since ICML renders verdicts on submitted manuscripts.

**Author rebuttal**: The authors provided strong rebuttals on: (1) Table 3 errors are exculpatory and the corrected values strengthen the paper's robustness claim; (2) RxR-CE comparison is statistically significant (z≈3.4, p<0.001); (3) the supervised-level framing is easily corrected by leading with the SpatialNav comparison; (4) ε=0.5m in implementation and will be specified; (5) code release is committed upon acceptance. The R2R-CE non-significance was acknowledged.

**Residual risk**: The adversarial's strongest point is that physical grounding is structurally undemonstrated in simulation (the waypoint predictor uses perfect depth). The author's rebuttal distinguishes scene reconstruction scale recovery (exercised in simulation) from waypoint predictor depth (uses simulator depth in simulation) — valid, but the waypoint predictor depth is the more navigation-critical use of physical grounding. The residual risk is that the physical grounding contribution cannot be independently verified from the submitted evaluation. The paper should add the suggested ablation or reframe physical grounding as a supporting component rather than a co-equal contribution.
